The Tale of Antivirus

Automata... Waaa?

To know why we have antivirus software we have to know about viruses.

The first published theory of a computer virus was in 1949 by Jhon von Neumann. The theory was focused on self replicating computer programs. Neumann called them Automana. This was prior to the internet. But, in 1971 the first self-replicating program, called Creeper built by Bob Thomas, was release on ARPANET. ARPANET was one of the first internets. The program was built in to "infect" specific computers running a specific operating system and had to be on ARPANET. Bob Thomas, being the nice guy that he was, also wrote the program to delete Creeper. It was, of course, called Reaper. This could be considered the first anti-virus, but technically it was a virus itself, written to delete Creeper.


The First Virus

Poetic Virus

The first known was written by a 15 year-old kid. Rick Skrenta wrote the program Elk Cloner as a joke in 1981. The virus would infect Apple DOS 3.3 systems by copping itself to the computer. When another floppy disk, remember those?!!, was inserted into the computer the virus would copy itself to the floppy. The virus was not intended to harm or to make a profit. So what did it do you ask? Tell you a poem, of course. On the 50th boot of the game it was attached to it would display a blank screen with a poem on it.

Elk Cloner:

The program with a personality

It will get on all your disks

It will infiltrate your chips

Yes, it's Cloner!

It will stick to you like glue

It will modify RAM too

Send in the Cloner!

The first virus "in the wild" was a called Brian. It was meant to track pirated software and lock the computer. It is considered the first MS-DOS virus.

The First Antivirus

One Program

The first antivirus was written by Bernd Fix. He wrote a program that remove one specific virus, Vienna Virus, in 1987. 1987 was the year the floodgates open for antivirus software. Many companies and software created then are still around today. See the list to the right of early antivirus names.
